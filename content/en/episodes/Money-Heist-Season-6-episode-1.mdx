## Why the Original Series Ended with Part 5

The decision to conclude the main series after five parts was a creative choice made by showrunner Álex Pina and the team at Vancouver Media. By the end of the second heist, the stakes had reached a point where any further escalation might have felt repetitive to the audience. The crew had already successfully infiltrated the Royal Mint of Spain and the Bank of Spain, two of the most secure locations in the country.

In the final episodes of Part 5, the Professor (Álvaro Morte) managed to negotiate a "peace treaty" with the Spanish government. The surviving members—including Lisbon, Rio, Denver, and Stockholm—were given new identities and a chance at a life away from crime. From a narrative perspective, starting a **Money Heist Season 6 episode 1** would require the Professor to lose his hard-earned freedom, which many fans feel would ruin the "happy ending" the characters finally achieved.

Furthermore, the death of Tokyo (Úrsula Corberó) served as a massive emotional anchor for the series finale. As the narrator and a fan-favorite lead, her absence would create a significant void in a potential sixth season. Most critics agree that ending the show while it was at its peak was a smarter move for the franchise’s long-term legacy than dragging out the plot until the quality began to dip.
